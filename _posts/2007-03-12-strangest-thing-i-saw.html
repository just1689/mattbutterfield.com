I was hunting for HDR images.
HDR stands for high dynamic range, which means it is a photo that has much more detail in overly dark and bright areas than a normal photo.
it is kind of hard to understand at first but...
you can make them in photoshop by combining a bunch of differently exposed images of the same scene into one image, so instead of getting something that looks like this:


you get this:

this is made from 12 different images, some overexposed, some underexposed to bring out allllll the detail possible.
as you can see there is a lot more detail overall, especially in the dark areas.
i think it is kind of cool, but they take a lot of time and work to make.
i would rather just go nuts and take a million photos of everything and not have to carry around a tripod and do a whole crapload of post-processing.
plus, even though this is how our eyes see the scene, it takes a lot of work to get them to look normal, and not like a drawing or something.
it's pretty weird.
